Broker Pre-Qualification Assessment, v2.0

A rebuilt version of the GHL intake quiz — every item closed-ended, every point auto-scorable, and the scoring model redesigned so it actually measures what predicts broker performance in a commission-only performance-marketing role.

PREPARED FOR: BROKER INTAKE / GHL AUTOMATION SCALE: 0–100 MANUAL REVIEW REQUIRED: NONE

What was wrong with v1

Four structural problems

  • One open-ended item broke the automation promise. V1's own design brief states no applicant should consume human time below a 70 score — but Q5 ("walk me through day one") required Alexis to read and grade free text before any borderline decision could be made. One write-in question means the "zero manual review" system was never actually zero-touch.
  • Passing a gate earned points. Q1 and Q2 were instant-disqualifiers, but answering "yes" to them also contributed 35 of the 100 possible points. That means two brokers with identical real competency could land on different sides of the qualified line just because one instrument conflated "meets the minimum requirement" with "is a strong candidate."
  • No coverage of the risks specific to this industry. The single highest-cost hiring mistake in performance marketing isn't a slow broker — it's one who's currently double-dipping with a competing network, or who was already terminated elsewhere for lead fraud or compliance violations. V1 never asked.
  • Self-report with no cross-check. Every score in V1 came from unverified self-report (Q3, Q4, Q7). There was no item that actually tests whether the candidate knows the tools or the compliance rules, versus just claims to.

V2 below fixes all four: gates carry no points, four new gates close the risk gaps, one item tests real tool fluency instead of self-report, and the one open-ended item is replaced with an auto-scored situational-judgment question.

SECTION AHard Eliminators

Pass/fail only — no points. A candidate must clear all seven before Section B is even scored. This is the one architectural change that matters most: table-stakes requirements no longer inflate the competency score.

A1Yes / No

Are you comfortable working in a commission-only structure with no base salary, where your income is 100% tied to accounts you activate and manage?

  • Yes continue
  • No auto-reject
A2Multiple choice

How many hours per week can you commit to this role for the first 30 days?

  • Under 10 hours auto-reject
  • 10–14 hours auto-reject
  • 15–19 hours continue
  • 20+ hours continue

Same 15-hour floor as V1, but the extra bands give you real availability data for onboarding load balancing instead of a bare yes/no.

A3Yes / No — new

This is a 1099 independent contractor role with no employee benefits. You are responsible for your own tax withholding, including quarterly estimated payments. Do you understand and accept this?

  • Yes continue
  • No auto-reject
A4Yes / No — new

Are you currently bound by a non-compete, exclusivity, or non-solicitation agreement with another performance marketing network, brokerage, or agency?

  • No continue
  • Yes auto-reject

Closes the single most expensive blind spot in V1. A broker who's contractually tied elsewhere creates conflict-of-interest and account-routing exposure the moment they're assigned advertisers.

A5Yes / No — new

Has any network, affiliate platform, or brokerage ever suspended or terminated an account of yours for fraud, lead quality, or compliance violations?

  • No continue
  • Yes auto-reject

Advertiser trust is the network's entire asset. One broker with a fraud history is a bigger liability than ten slow ones — V1 had no question that would have caught this.

A6Yes / No

Are you willing to submit a weekly performance report every Monday by 10 AM without exception, including weeks where results are low?

  • Yes, no problem continue
  • Yes, but may need reminders auto-reject
  • No / depends auto-reject
A7Yes / No — new

Can you guarantee a response to advertiser or publisher contacts within 4 business hours, Monday–Friday, during your stated availability window?

  • Yes continue
  • No auto-reject

V1 only stated the 4-hour SLA in the closing script of the live interview — after the intake quiz. Moving it into the gate means nobody reaches a video call unqualified on a requirement they never actually agreed to.

SECTION BScored Competency — 90 points

Applies only to candidates who cleared every Section A gate. Every item is multiple choice or multi-select; nothing here requires a human to read prose and assign a grade.

B1Multiple choice · 0–20 pts

Have you previously worked within a CRM or tracking system where daily activity logging was required? (e.g. Salesforce, HubSpot, GoHighLevel, Pipedrive)

  • Yes, used daily 20
  • Yes, occasionally 11
  • No, but willing to learn 5
  • No 0
B2Multiple choice · 0–20 pts

In your most recent role or business, what was your average monthly commission or performance-based earnings?

  • $5,000+ / mo 20
  • $2,000–$4,999 / mo 13
  • $500–$1,999 / mo 7
  • Under $500 / N/A 0
B3Multi-select · 0–15 pts — new

Which of the following tracking platforms have you personally configured postbacks or pixels in? Select all that apply.

  • Everflow / HasOffers (Tune) / Cake 3 pts each
  • Linktrust / Offer18 / Hitpath 3 pts each
  • None of the above 0

Capped at 15. Replaces a vague "system experience" self-rating with a check against the actual platforms this network runs on — a candidate either knows the tools or doesn't.

B4Situational judgment · 0–25 pts — replaces old Q5

You're assigned three new advertiser accounts that must be live and producing within 7 days. What do you do on day one?

  • Contact all three advertiser/publisher accounts same day to confirm contacts and tracking requirements, then log a 7-day activation checklist 25
  • Start with whichever account looks easiest to activate first, plan the other two once it's live 13
  • Read the full SOP end to end before contacting anyone, even if it pushes outreach past day one 8
  • Wait for the account contacts to reach out first so I understand what they need 0

Same construct V1's Q5 was reaching for — process thinking under a deadline — but with answer options ranked in advance instead of a paragraph someone has to read and judge. Fully removes the one manual-review step in the entire funnel.

B5Multi-select · 0–10 pts

What performance marketing verticals have you worked in? Select all that apply.

  • Insurance / FE / Medicare 5 pts
  • Solar / Home Services 5 pts
  • Legal / Mass Tort 5 pts
  • Debt / Financial 5 pts
  • None 0

Unchanged from V1 — capped at 10 regardless of how many are selected.

SECTION CIntegrity Check — 10 points

C1Instructed response — new

To confirm you're reading each question fully rather than clicking through, select "Legal / Mass Tort" as your answer to this item.

  • Legal / Mass Tort (the instructed answer) 10, continue
  • Any other answer auto-reject, tag: careless-submission

Standard survey-integrity technique: an instructed-response item catches bots, click-farmed applications, and candidates speed-running the quiz without reading it — all of which quietly inflate a "qualified" pool with noise. Rotate the instructed vertical/phrase periodically so it can't be memorized and shared.

Updated scoring rubric

Same four-band structure as V1, but nothing below it requires Alexis to read free text — even the borderline band is now driven entirely by the Section B/C score.

Automated action by total Section B + C score (0–100)
ScoreClassificationAutomated action
0–49Not qualifiedAuto-reject email. Tag: scored-low. 90-day re-application window.
50–69BorderlineAuto-routed to a short supplemental video-response round (Part 2 of the interview script, below) before a scheduling decision. No manual text grading — the video round replaces it.
70–84QualifiedCalendar link auto-sent. Tag: qualified. Assigned to junior broker for pre-call prep.
85–100High prioritySame as qualified + immediate Teams ping to Alexis. Fast-track review.

Any single Section A failure (A1–A7) overrides all of the above — reject fires immediately regardless of when the candidate would have reached Section B.

What to leave alone

Keep the live video interview open-ended

The five-question interview script (Task 3 in the original doc) is a different instrument doing a different job, and it should stay qualitative. A situational-judgment survey is built to filter at scale before any human time is spent; the interview exists for the opposite reason — to observe how someone constructs an answer under mild pressure, not just which option they'd pick. Forcing those five questions into multiple choice would remove exactly the signal they're designed to capture: hesitation, defensiveness, whether they can build a specific example versus a generic one.

If you want more consistency without losing that signal, don't convert the questions — standardize the scoring. Give whoever conducts the interview a 0–3 rating per question (0 = red flag as written in the original script, 1 = weak, 2 = solid, 3 = exceptional) and log it as a number in GHL alongside the intake score. That gives you a combined quantitative view without pretending a behavioral interview is a quiz.

Implementation notes for GHL

  • Add four new custom fields for the Section A gates that didn't exist in V1: TAX_ACK, NONCOMPETE_FLAG, FRAUD_HISTORY_FLAG, SLA_ACK.
  • Rebuild the INTAKE_SCORE calculation to sum only Section B + C (previously it included Q1/Q2 gate points).
  • Delete the "default score 8 until reviewed" logic on old Q5 — B6 auto-scores immediately, so the borderline branch no longer waits on manual input.
  • Add the instructed-response check on C1 as a rejection branch parallel to the existing eliminator checks.
  • Rotate the C1 instructed phrase quarterly and keep a private log of which phrase was live for which applicant batch, in case of disputes.
